Exercise? Circus arts were originally a form of entertainment and have evolved over the years into an effective form of exercise. Each of the apparatuses provides a unique workout that targets, strengthens and stretches muscle groups.

Apparatuses

Trapeze

A trapeze is a short horizontal bar hung by ropes or metal straps from a support. Similar to a pull-up bar this provides a full upper body workout

Chinese Pole

Chinese poles are vertical poles on which circus performers climb, slide down and hold poses. The poles are sometimes coated in rubber to improve grip. Chinese pole provides a full body workout with emphasis on the shoulders and back.

Tight wire

Tightrope walking is the art of walking along a thin wire or rope. It typically targets the core and lower body. It improves one’s overall muscle control and balance

Cord lisse

The name is French for “smooth rope”. It is the rope we all remember from P.E class. Similar to the Chinese pole, it provides a full body workout.

Juggling

Juggling is a physical skill involving the manipulation of objects for recreation, entertainment or sport. The most recognizable form of juggling is toss juggling. This is meant to exercise your brain.
